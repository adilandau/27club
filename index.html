They lived fast,
burned bright,
and died at 27.

The 27 Club is a term that refers to a group of extraordinarily influential musicians who all died at the age of twenty-seven. From Robert Johnson at a Mississippi crossroads in 1938 to Amy Winehouse in north London in 2011, what unites them is not only their age, but the extraordinary mark each left on music history — a legacy far larger than a life so brief should allow.
