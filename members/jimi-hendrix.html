Early Life

Seattle

James Marshall Hendrix was born on November 27, 1942, in Seattle, Washington, to Al and Lucille Hendrix. His childhood was turbulent — his parents divorced, his mother died when he was fifteen, and he and his siblings were shuffled between relatives. He taught himself guitar on a ukulele, then a one-string acoustic, before his father finally bought him a proper guitar at sixteen.

He served briefly in the U.S. Army as a paratrooper but was discharged in 1962, reportedly for injuries sustained in a parachute drop. He then spent years playing backing guitar for R&B artists including Little Richard and the Isley Brothers, developing his extraordinary technique in obscurity.

Legacy

The Greatest

Jimi Hendrix is widely regarded as the greatest electric guitarist in history. In just four years of fame — between 1966 and 1970 — he redefined what the instrument could do. He used feedback, distortion, and whammy bar not as gimmicks but as expressive vocabulary. He played with his teeth, behind his back, and famously set his guitar on fire at the Monterey Pop Festival in 1967.

His performance of "The Star-Spangled Banner" at Woodstock in 1969 — alone, with no band, turning the national anthem into a sonic portrait of war and protest — remains one of the defining moments in the history of American music.

Music Career

Electric Revolution

Discovered in New York by Chas Chandler in 1966, Hendrix was brought to London where the Jimi Hendrix Experience was formed. Their debut single "Hey Joe" charted in the UK, followed by "Purple Haze" — one of rock's defining songs. Their debut album Are You Experienced (1967) was a revelation, blending blues structure with psychedelic experimentation and visceral guitar playing.

He followed up with Axis: Bold as Love (1967) and Electric Ladyland (1968), the latter a sprawling double album that remains one of rock's masterpieces. He died on September 18, 1970, in London, of asphyxiation after a barbiturate overdose, at the age of twenty-seven. He had been about to embark on a new musical direction.
